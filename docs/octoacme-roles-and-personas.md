# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Engineers

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Engineers ensure product quality through comprehensive testing strategies, test automation, and quality metrics tracking. They collaborate with developers and product managers to define quality standards and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans and test cases
- Develop and maintain automated test suites
- Perform manual testing for exploratory and edge cases
- Track and report quality metrics and defect trends
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria definition
- Validate releases before deployment

### Goals
- Maintain high product quality and reliability
- Reduce defect escape rate to production
- Increase test automation coverage
- Enable fast, confident releases

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Bug reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Test results and release sign-off reports
- Participation in sprint planning and retrospectives

---

## Tech Leads

### Role Summary
Tech Leads provide technical direction, architectural guidance, and mentor development teams. They bridge technical decisions with business needs and ensure engineering best practices.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical architecture and design patterns
- Review and approve technical designs
- Mentor developers and conduct code reviews
- Make technical trade-off decisions
- Ensure code quality and engineering standards
- Identify and mitigate technical debt

### Goals
- Maintain technical excellence and architectural integrity
- Enable team productivity through clear technical direction
- Balance innovation with maintainability
- Foster technical growth within the team

### Typical Communication
- Technical design documents and architecture decision records
- Code review feedback and technical guidance
- Technical roadmap discussions
- Engineering team meetings and tech talks

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate and execute software releases, ensuring smooth deployments with minimal risk. They manage release schedules, deployment procedures, and post-release validation.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and schedule releases
- Coordinate release activities across teams
- Manage release checklists and deployment procedures
- Monitor deployments and rollback if needed
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Track release metrics and improve processes

### Goals
- Achieve zero-downtime deployments
- Minimize release-related incidents
- Reduce release cycle time
- Ensure release predictability and reliability

### Typical Communication
- Release plans and schedules
- Deployment runbooks and checklists
- Release notes and change logs
- Post-release reports and metrics

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are individuals or groups with vested interest in project outcomes. They may include executives, customers, business unit leaders, or other teams that depend on or are affected by the project.

### Responsibilities
- Provide input on business requirements and priorities
- Review and approve project milestones and deliverables
- Allocate resources and budget
- Make key business decisions
- Provide feedback on project direction

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with business objectives
- Maximize return on investment
- Stay informed on project progress and risks
- Influence project direction based on business needs

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder briefings and status updates
- Project review meetings and steering committees
- Sign-offs on major milestones and decisions
- Escalation and risk discussions
