# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Clarifies requirements, estimates effort, proposes technical solutions
- **UX Lead**: Implements designs, provides feedback on feasibility and technical constraints
- **QA Specialist**: Collaborates on testability, fixes defects, writes automated tests
- **Project Managers**: Reports progress, raises blockers, assists in planning and estimation
- **Data Analyst**: Implements instrumentation and data collection logic

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Defines requirements, prioritizes backlog, validates delivered features
- **UX Lead**: Collaborates on user research, validates designs align with product vision
- **Data Analyst**: Defines success metrics, reviews impact data, makes prioritization decisions
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on scope and timeline, balances priorities with constraints
- **Change Manager**: Communicates feature value and benefits to support adoption

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on priorities, manages scope and timeline expectations
- **Developers**: Tracks progress, removes blockers, coordinates cross-team dependencies
- **Change Manager**: Coordinates release planning, ensures stakeholder readiness
- **QA Specialist**: Monitors quality metrics, ensures testing timelines are met
- **Data Analyst**: Reviews progress metrics, identifies delivery trends and risks

---

## UX Lead

### Role Summary
UX Leads ensure products are user-centered, accessible, and intuitive. They conduct research, design interfaces, validate usability, and advocate for the end-user experience throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design user interfaces, wireframes, and prototypes
- Collaborate with Product Managers to define user stories and acceptance criteria
- Work with Developers to ensure designs are implemented correctly
- Maintain design systems and accessibility standards
- Validate features against user experience goals

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive and accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction metrics
- Ensure design consistency across features

### Typical Communication
- Weekly design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Design specifications and prototype walkthroughs
- Accessibility audit results

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on user stories, validates problem statements with user research
- **Developers**: Provides design specs, reviews implementations, clarifies design intent
- **QA Specialist**: Partners on usability testing and acceptance criteria validation
- **Project Managers**: Reports on design milestones and identifies design-related risks

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts transform raw data into actionable insights that inform product decisions, measure success, and identify opportunities for improvement. They support evidence-based decision making across the organization.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Build dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Analyze user behavior and feature adoption
- Support A/B testing and experimentation
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities in data
- Collaborate with teams to define success metrics

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Measure and communicate product impact
- Identify opportunities for optimization

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews with Product and Project Managers
- Data findings presentations to stakeholders
- Documentation of data models and reporting logic
- Ad-hoc analyses for feature validation

### Key Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Defines success metrics, provides impact analysis, supports prioritization decisions
- **Developers**: Collaborates on instrumentation and data collection requirements
- **Project Managers**: Reports on progress metrics, identifies data-driven risks
- **Stakeholders**: Delivers insights and recommendations through reports and dashboards

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers guide organizations through transitions, ensuring stakeholders are prepared, trained, and supported when new features or processes are introduced. They minimize disruption and maximize adoption.

### Responsibilities
- Assess change impact on different user groups and stakeholders
- Develop change management and communication plans
- Create training materials and conduct training sessions
- Coordinate with stakeholders to ensure readiness
- Monitor adoption and address resistance
- Capture feedback and facilitate continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize user adoption of new features and processes
- Minimize disruption during transitions
- Ensure stakeholders are informed and prepared

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder readiness assessments and reports
- Training schedules and materials
- Change impact analyses
- Adoption metrics and feedback summaries

### Key Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on release timelines, identifies change-related risks, coordinates stakeholder communication
- **Product Managers**: Understands feature value proposition, helps craft messaging for end users
- **Developers**: Learns technical details to create accurate training materials
- **QA Specialist**: Validates that training materials reflect actual product behavior

---

## QA Specialist

### Role Summary
QA Specialists ensure product quality through systematic testing, defect identification, and validation of acceptance criteria. They advocate for quality throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans and test cases
- Perform functional, regression, and exploratory testing
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Validate acceptance criteria are met before release
- Collaborate with Developers on bug triage and resolution
- Maintain test automation where applicable

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free releases
- Catch issues before they reach production
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Daily standup updates on testing progress and blockers
- Bug reports and defect triage sessions
- Test plan reviews and coverage reports
- Sign-off on release readiness

### Key Interactions
- **Developers**: Reports bugs, collaborates on reproduction steps, validates fixes
- **Product Managers**: Validates features meet acceptance criteria and user expectations
- **Project Managers**: Reports on quality metrics, identifies testing-related risks and timeline impacts
- **UX Lead**: Conducts usability testing, validates user experience requirements
