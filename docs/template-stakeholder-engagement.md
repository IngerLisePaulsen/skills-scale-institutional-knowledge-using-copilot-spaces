| Support Team | Training & Changes | Before Release | Workshop | PM/Tech Lead |

### Communication Templates

#### Executive Status Update
- **Audience:** Executive stakeholders, high power/low interest
- **Frequency:** Bi-weekly or monthly
- **Format:** Concise email (1 page max) or dashboard
- **Content:**
  - Project health status (on track, at risk, off track)
  - Key accomplishments since last update
  - Major milestones upcoming
  - Critical risks or decisions needed
  - Budget status

#### Detailed Progress Report
- **Audience:** Product owners, project stakeholders
- **Frequency:** Weekly
- **Format:** Email or shared document
- **Content:**
  - Sprint/iteration progress
  - Completed user stories/features
  - Upcoming work
  - Blockers and dependencies
  - Risk updates
  - Metrics and trends

#### Release Communication
- **Audience:** End users, customer-facing teams
- **Frequency:** Per release
- **Format:** Release notes, email, portal announcement
- **Content:**
  - New features and enhancements
  - Bug fixes and improvements
  - Known issues and workarounds
  - Migration/upgrade instructions
  - Support contact information

## Stakeholder Engagement Strategies

### For High Power, High Interest Stakeholders
- **Engage Closely:**
  - Schedule regular 1:1 meetings
  - Involve in key decisions and trade-offs
  - Seek input on direction and priorities
  - Share detailed information proactively
  - Address concerns immediately

### For High Power, Low Interest Stakeholders
- **Keep Satisfied:**
  - Provide executive summaries
  - Highlight key milestones and outcomes
  - Minimize time demands
  - Escalate only critical issues
  - Respect their time and attention

### For Low Power, High Interest Stakeholders
- **Keep Informed:**
  - Provide regular updates
  - Share detailed progress and plans
  - Welcome their input and feedback
  - Include in demos and showcases
  - Build advocates and champions

### For Low Power, Low Interest Stakeholders
- **Monitor:**
  - Send periodic general updates
  - Keep them aware of major changes
  - Don't over-communicate
  - Watch for changes in interest or power
  - Include in broad announcements

## Managing Difficult Situations

### Conflicting Stakeholder Priorities
- [ ] Document all perspectives
- [ ] Facilitate discussion to find common ground
- [ ] Escalate to decision authority if needed
- [ ] Communicate decision rationale clearly
- [ ] Follow up with all parties

### Scope Creep Requests
- [ ] Acknowledge the request
- [ ] Assess impact on timeline, budget, resources
- [ ] Present trade-offs clearly
- [ ] Seek formal approval for changes
- [ ] Update project plan and communicate

### Stakeholder Resistance or Concerns
- [ ] Listen actively and understand concerns
- [ ] Provide data and evidence
- [ ] Involve them in solution finding
- [ ] Address misconceptions promptly
- [ ] Escalate if needed

## Escalation Path

### Level 1: Project Manager
- Routine updates and questions
- Day-to-day coordination
- Standard decisions within project scope

### Level 2: Product Owner / Tech Lead
- Priority conflicts
- Scope or requirement changes
- Technical trade-off decisions

### Level 3: Executive Sponsor
- Major budget or timeline changes
- Critical risks or blockers
- Strategic direction changes
- Cross-organizational conflicts

### Escalation Guidelines
- [ ] Escalation criteria defined
- [ ] Escalation process documented
- [ ] Contact information for all levels maintained
- [ ] Response time expectations set
- [ ] Escalation log maintained
